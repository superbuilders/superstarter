## 1. Why this round, why now

Three forcing functions:

- **The real CCAT placement exam doesn't have synonyms.** Across the 65 captured items in `data/testbank/12min_prep_practice_{1,2}` (the OCR'd subset of two of six prep practice tests), `verbal.synonyms` has zero observations. The existing v1 taxonomy treated synonyms as a first-class sub-type; the empirical signal from the actual placement-exam-style items says it isn't worth a dedicated sub-type slot in the diagnostic mix or the seed bank. Cutting synonyms before the testbank-re-extraction round means the OCR pipeline never produces synonyms-tagged items.

- **The numerical taxonomy is too coarse.** The existing `numerical.word_problems` sub-type sweeps too many distinct problem-solving patterns into one bucket — workrate problems, speed/distance/time triads, and lowest-value comparisons all read as "word problems" under the old taxonomy but reward different recognition skills and deserve independent mastery state. Splitting `numerical.averages_ratios` into `numerical.averages` + `numerical.ratios` (the two have different recognition shapes — averages keys on sum-over-count or delta-from-mean; ratios keys on parts-to-parts vs parts-to-whole) and adding the three new numerical sub-types tightens the mastery model's per-sub-type signal-to-noise.

- **`verbal.logic` understates the sub-type's content.** The CCAT taxonomy's "logic" sub-type covers syllogisms, spatial-direction problems, and short-passage critical reasoning; in the existing v1 taxonomy, `verbal.logic` was the catch-all for all three, and the SPEC's threshold-list framing categorized it as 18s sustained-multi-constraint reasoning. Renaming to `verbal.critical_reasoning` aligns the id with the dominant pattern — critical reasoning subsumes syllogisms and spatial-direction problems as special cases, not the other way around. The 18s threshold and section ("verbal" — the code was already correct here per audit Q5) are unchanged.
